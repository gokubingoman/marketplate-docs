## Verification Requirements

Verification is a gate, not a formality. Unverified creators cannot publish paid listings or accept orders. The flow:

```
Signup → Identity verification → Kitchen verification → Compliance → Verified Creator
```

### Identity verification

Confirms you are a real, accountable operator.

| Required | Entity type |
|----------|-------------|
| Government-issued photo ID | All |
| Selfie / liveness check | All |
| Business registration | LLC, partnership |
| EIN / tax identity | All (where applicable) |
| Phone verification | All |

Review timeline: typically within 2 business days. Status updates appear in [Compliance](../../pages/creator/compliance.md) and via email.

### Kitchen verification

Confirms where food is produced. Every menu item must link to a verified production location.

| Kitchen type | Typical use |
|--------------|-------------|
| Home / cottage kitchen | Cottage food operators |
| Commercial shared kitchen | Independent chefs, meal prep |
| Dedicated commercial facility | Scale operators, bakers |
| Commissary | Food trucks |
| Multi-tenant bay | Commercial kitchen tenants (link to pre-verified facility) |

Submit production address, facility photos (prep area, storage, handwashing), inspection records where available, and facility registration.

### Compliance verification

Jurisdiction-specific licenses, permits, and food safety documents. Required items vary by state and locality — your checklist is generated at signup based on your jurisdiction and creator category.

Common documents: food handler certificate, business license, cottage food registration, liability insurance (where required).

## Food Safety Requirements

Marketplate verifies documentation and consistency. Government authorities retain regulatory authority — we are not a substitute for health inspections or legal counsel.

### Your responsibilities

| Area | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Accurate allergen disclosure** | Every listing must declare allergens completely; incomplete data blocks publish |
| **Ingredient transparency** | Full ingredient lists required; no vague "house blend" without disclosure |
| **Category compliance** | Platform enforces prohibited items per jurisdiction (e.g., certain refrigerated items under cottage food rules) |
| **Document currency** | Renew food handler certs, licenses, and registrations before expiration |
| **Production location accuracy** | Every SKU linked to your verified kitchen; do not produce in unverified locations |
| **Safe handling at handoff** | Follow temperature and time standards for pickup and delivery |

### Cottage food operators

Enhanced rules apply: category restrictions enforced in the [Menu Item Editor](../../pages/creator/menu-item-editor.md), sales cap tracking where jurisdictions impose limits (warnings at 80%, block at 100%), and label requirements on physical packaging.

Violations — especially confirmed allergen mislabeling — result in immediate listing suspension pending investigation.

## Photography Guide

Food photography is your primary sales tool on Marketplate. Discovery is photography-forward; the UI stays neutral so your images carry visual weight. Poor photography reduces conversion and, over time, discovery eligibility.

### Standards

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Real food only** | Photograph what you actually sell — no stock imagery, no AI-generated food |
| **Primary photo required** | Every published listing needs at least one high-quality primary image |
| **Accurate representation** | Portion size, plating, and garnishes should match what customers receive |
| **Minimum resolution** | Upload JPG or PNG at least 1200px on the longest edge |
| **Maximum 5 photos per item** | Use additional photos for angles, cross-sections, or packaging |
| **Clean background** | Neutral surfaces or contextual kitchen settings; avoid cluttered backgrounds |
| **Natural light preferred** | Avoid heavy filters that misrepresent color or texture |

### Composition tips

1. **Shoot at plate level** — not overhead-only unless the dish benefits from it (pizza, boards, spreads)
2. **Show texture** — steam, crumb, glaze, and layers sell better than flat compositions
3. **Include context sparingly** — a hand, utensil, or napkin adds scale without distraction
4. **Match your brand** — consistent styling across your catalog builds storefront cohesion
5. **Update seasonally** — swap photos when recipes or presentation change

### What to avoid

- Stock photography (detected and rejected)
- Misleading garnishes you do not include in the order
- Dark, blurry, or flash-washed images
- Photos with visible competitor branding or unrelated logos

Photography quality feeds discovery ranking over time. Featured collections require minimum quality thresholds.

→ Design context: [Design Principles — Visual hierarchy](../../design-system/principles.md) · [Menu Item Editor — Photos](../../pages/creator/menu-item-editor.md)

## Packaging Guide

How food travels from your kitchen to your customer is part of the product. Bad packaging creates refunds, bad reviews, and trust incidents.

### General principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Temperature integrity** | Hot food hot, cold food cold — use insulated bags, heat packs, or ice packs as appropriate |
| **Leak prevention** | Sauces in sealed containers; soups in tamper-evident cups; double-bag liquids |
| **Structural protection** | Cakes in rigid boxes; delicate items secured against sliding |
| **Label clarity** | Item name, allergens, pickup/ consumption instructions, your business name |
| **Tamper evidence** | Stickers or seals on bags so customers know food was not opened in transit |

### By fulfillment type

| Type | Packaging focus |
|------|-----------------|
| **Pickup — scheduled window** | Label with customer name and order number; group items by order |
| **Pickup — same day / food truck** | Minimal packaging optimized for speed; clear handoff identification |
| **Delivery — creator managed** | Insulated transport; secure containers that survive 15–30 minute trips |
| **Meal prep batches** | Date labels, reheating instructions, macro/dietary labels if advertised |
| **Shipping — non-perishable** | Only where legally permitted; crush-proof outer box, inner padding |

### Cottage food labeling

Follow your jurisdiction's cottage food label requirements. At minimum, include: product name, ingredient list, allergen declaration, net weight, your name and address, and required jurisdictional statements.

## Pricing Recommendations

You own your pricing strategy. Marketplate does not force race-to-bottom discounts. These guidelines help you price sustainably.

### Cost-based foundation

Calculate your true cost per unit:

```
Food cost + packaging + labor + overhead + platform fee + payment processing ≈ floor price
```

Add margin for profit, waste, and growth. If you cannot articulate your floor price, you are likely undercharging.

### Pricing models on Marketplate

| Model | Best for | Checkout behavior |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| **Fixed** | Standard menu items with known cost | Customer pays listed price |
| **Starting at** | Items with optional add-ons or size tiers | Base price + variant selection |
| **Custom quote** | Catering, large events, bespoke orders | Inquiry or deposit flow |

Configure per item in the [Menu Item Editor](../../pages/creator/menu-item-editor.md).

### Practical recommendations

| Tactic | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Price for pickup efficiency** | Pickup orders cost you less than delivery — consider different pricing or minimums |
| **Bundle for AOV** | Family packs, weekly meal bundles, and dessert add-ons increase average order value |
| **Set minimum order values for delivery** | Protects margin on low-ticket delivery runs |
| **Use lead times strategically** | Custom or batch items with 24–72 hour lead times reduce waste and justify premium pricing |
| **Review quarterly** | Ingredient costs shift; stale pricing erodes margin silently |

Do not invent fee numbers in customer-facing copy. Platform fees are disclosed transparently at checkout.

## Getting Repeat Customers

One-time buyers prove discovery works. Repeat buyers prove your business works.

### How repeat purchase works on Marketplate

Customers can favorite your storefront, reorder from order history, and receive notifications when you publish new menus or open new pickup windows. Your operational reliability and review scores influence whether they return.

### Tactics that drive repeat orders

| Tactic | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Consistent schedule** | Same pickup day each week builds habit (e.g., "Saturday bread drop") |
| **Rotating menu with anchors** | Change specials; keep 1–2 signature items always available |
| **Post-fulfillment follow-up** | Thank customers in [Messages](../../pages/creator/messages.md); mention next week's menu |
| **Quality consistency** | Batch production standards so the second order matches the first |
| **Loyalty through quality, not discounts** | Trust-seeking customers return for reliability, not coupons |

**Target metric:** Track repeat customer rate in [Analytics](../../pages/creator/analytics.md). Aim for improvement month over month after your first 20 orders.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Getting started

**How long does verification take?**  
Identity and kitchen reviews typically complete within 2 business days each. Compliance depends on how quickly you upload required documents. Total time from signup to verified status is often 3–7 business days.

**Can I build my menu before verification completes?**  
Yes. Save drafts in [Catalog](../../pages/creator/catalog.md). Publish is disabled until verification completes — with a clear explanation, not a silent block.

**What if my verification is rejected?**  
You receive a specific reason and a path to revise and resubmit. Appeals are available for edge cases.

### Operations

**What happens if I sell out?**  
Mark items or windows sold out in [Availability](../../pages/creator/availability.md) or the Menu Item Editor. The system blocks checkout when capacity is zero — overselling is a platform defect, not your problem to manage manually.

**Can I pause my storefront?**  
Yes. Set blackout dates, mark items sold out, or disable fulfillment types. Do not leave a live storefront accepting orders you cannot fulfill.

**How do cancellations work?**  
Creator-initiated cancellations issue full refunds and impact your reliability metrics. Customer cancellations follow your published policy, visible at checkout.

### Payments

**When do I get paid?**  
Payout schedule is visible in [Payouts](../../pages/creator/payouts.md) after Stripe connection. Pending balances settle after order completion per platform rules.

**Why is my payout on hold?**  
Common reasons: expired compliance document, active dispute, or incomplete Stripe setup. The dashboard explains the specific hold — never silent.

### Trust & safety

**Can I remove a bad review?**  
No. Legitimate reviews stay. Flag reviews that violate policy (harassment, not from a verified purchase, fraudulent) for moderation review.

**What happens when my food handler certificate expires?**  
Reminders at 30, 14, and 7 days. After grace period, listings suspend until you upload a current document.
