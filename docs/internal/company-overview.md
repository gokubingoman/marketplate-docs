## What Marketplate Is

Marketplate is building the **trusted marketplace and operating system for independent food creators** — independent chefs, meal prep businesses, bakers, caterers, food truck operators, cottage food operators, pop-up kitchens, commercial kitchen tenants, and other local food entrepreneurs who sell directly to customers.

We combine two layers into one product:

| Layer | Role |
|-------|------|
| **Marketplace** | Where verified creators are discovered, evaluated, and purchased from by trust-seeking customers |
| **Operating system** | The software creators use to run their business — catalog, orders, fulfillment, customer communication, compliance, and analytics |

Marketplate is **creator infrastructure**, not another delivery aggregator. We do not optimize for anonymous restaurant supply, 30-minute delivery arbitrage, or commission extraction from existing brick-and-mortar restaurants. See [Product Overview — What Marketplate Is Not](../../product/overview.md#what-marketplate-is-not) and [Market Context — What Marketplate Is Not](../../company/market-context.md#what-marketplate-is-not).

## Why Marketplate Exists

### The Problem

Independent food entrepreneurship is larger and more fragmented than the "restaurant industry" lens captures. Creators across meal prep, baking, catering, food trucks, cottage food, pop-ups, and commercial kitchens need business infrastructure — not another listing on a delivery app that treats them as interchangeable supply.

Today, creators stitch together Instagram, Square, Google Sheets, WhatsApp, and duct-taped tools. Customers want to buy local food with confidence but lack a trust layer for anonymous online sellers. Delivery aggregators verify restaurants once at onboarding and hide details from customers. Generic e-commerce platforms ignore food safety, kitchen verification, and perishable fulfillment.

Four structural gaps define the opportunity — detailed in [Market Context](../../company/market-context.md#the-market-gap):

1. **Trust without enterprise overhead** — No incumbent solves both creator signaling and customer confidence
2. **Operating system, not just listings** — Creators need integrated order, fulfillment, compliance, and reputation management
3. **Creator business models, not restaurant assumptions** — Subscriptions, pre-orders, catering quotes, pop-ups, and shipping coexist
4. **Local food economy coordination** — Commercial kitchens, regulators, and fulfillment partners lack shared platform infrastructure

### The Trust Thesis

**Trust is our product. Software enables trust.**

Every feature, policy, screen, and workflow exists to answer one question for the customer: *Can I trust this creator with my food, my health, and my money?*

Customers should feel they are buying from **verified creators**, not anonymous sellers. That feeling is the outcome of identity verification, kitchen verification, transparent labeling, food safety compliance, honest reviews, and operational reliability working together as a system.

When trust and growth conflict, we choose trust. A slower path to scale with verified creators is preferable to a fast path built on unverified supply. See [Mission — The Trust Thesis](../../company/mission.md#the-trust-thesis).

## Mission and Vision

### Mission

> Empower independent food entrepreneurs with world-class software.

Marketplate exists to give independent food creators the same caliber of software that large restaurant chains and delivery aggregators reserve for themselves — without forcing creators to become technologists, logistics companies, or compliance departments. Full mission statement: [Mission](../../company/mission.md).

### Vision

> Become the trusted marketplace operating system for independent food creators — the infrastructure layer for the local food economy.

In ten years, when an independent chef, baker, or meal prep operator starts a food business anywhere Marketplate operates, the default path is: **verify identity, verify kitchen, launch on Marketplate, grow a trusted customer base.**

Five strategic pillars define the 5–10 year horizon — see [Vision — Strategic Pillars](../../company/vision.md#strategic-pillars-510-year-horizon):

1. **Trusted Marketplace OS** — Creators manage discovery, commerce, fulfillment, compliance, and customer relationships in one platform
2. **Verification as Competitive Moat** — Identity, kitchen, and compliance verification become harder to replicate over time
3. **International Scale with Local Integrity** — Geographic expansion respects local food law and cultural norms
4. **Infrastructure Layer for the Local Food Economy** — Commercial kitchens, commissaries, and fulfillment partners integrate natively
5. **AI-Augmented Operations at Scale** — AI removes repetitive work; humans remain accountable for trust-critical decisions

### What We Will Not Become

| Anti-vision | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| A delivery aggregator competing on speed and commission | Commoditizes creators; optimizes for logistics arbitrage, not trust |
| A generic SMB storefront builder | Ignores food-specific compliance, verification, and fulfillment complexity |
| A race-to-the-bottom marketplace | Volume without verification erodes the trust thesis |
| A feature-bloated "everything app" | Complexity destroys clarity; we build fewer things exceptionally well |

## Competitive Landscape

### Platform Comparison

| Dimension | Delivery Aggregators | Generic E-commerce | Marketplate |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Primary user | Restaurant owner | Any seller | Independent food creator |
| Trust model | Opaque, minimal | Buyer/seller reviews only | Identity + kitchen + compliance verification |
| Fulfillment | Platform delivery | Seller-managed | Creator-configured with platform coordination |
| Business tools | Menu management | Storefront + payments | Full food creator OS |
| Customer relationship | Platform-owned | Seller-owned | Creator-owned with platform trust layer |
| Food safety | Regulatory minimum | Not addressed | Core product pillar |

### Delivery Aggregators vs. Creator Infrastructure

```
Delivery Aggregator Model          Creator Infrastructure Model (Marketplate)
─────────────────────────          ──────────────────────────────────────────
Supply: existing restaurants       Supply: independent food creators
Goal: move orders fast             Goal: build trusted businesses
Customer relationship: platform    Customer relationship: creator-first
Verification: minimal, opaque      Verification: core product, transparent
Creator tools: menu + tablet       Creator tools: full business OS
Fulfillment: platform-controlled   Fulfillment: creator-configured
Trust signal: star rating          Trust signal: identity + kitchen + compliance + community
```

Aggregators will not fill this gap — their unit economics depend on high-volume restaurant supply and delivery logistics margin. Marketplate does not need to out-deliver DoorDash. We need to out-trust, out-tool, and out-serve independent creators. Full competitive framing: [Market Context](../../company/market-context.md).

## Marketplace Strategy

### Platform Invariants

| Invariant | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| **Verified to sell** | Unverified creators cannot accept paid orders |
| **Transparent to buy** | Customers see trust-relevant information before payment |
| **Creator-owned relationship** | Platform facilitates; creator is merchant of record |
| **Audit everything** | Trust, payment, and moderation actions leave immutable audit trails |
| **Human approval on high stakes** | AI recommends; humans approve verification, suspensions, and policy exceptions |

### Trust Model

Trust operates as a coherent system with six layers — each reinforcing the others:

1. **Identity verification** — Real, accountable operators
2. **Kitchen verification** — Approved production environments
3. **Compliance verification** — Jurisdiction-appropriate licenses and permits
4. **Listing transparency** — Ingredients, allergens, fulfillment, policies visible pre-payment
5. **Reviews & community** — Verified-purchase-only reputation
6. **Platform integrity** — Moderation, dispute resolution, progressive enforcement

Progressive enforcement ladder: Education → Warning → Listing restriction → Order suspension → Account suspension → Permanent removal. Full trust model: [Marketplace Mechanics — Trust Model](../../product/marketplace-mechanics.md#trust-model).

### Discovery Strategy

Discovery optimizes for **fit and confidence**, not engagement maximization. Ranking priorities (in order):

1. Verification and compliance status
2. Relevance to query and filters
3. Quality signals (reviews, completion rate, dispute rate)
4. Proximity and availability
5. Personalization (opt-in where required)

**Prohibited:** Paid promotion that obscures verification status; boosting creators with expired compliance. See [Marketplace Mechanics — Discovery](../../product/marketplace-mechanics.md#discovery).

### Fulfillment Strategy

Marketplate supports diverse fulfillment models because independent creators operate differently:

| Model | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| Pickup — scheduled window | Baker, meal prep, chef |
| Pickup — same day / ASAP | Food truck, pop-up |
| Delivery — creator managed | Chef, caterer |
| Delivery — partner/coordinated | Meal prep at scale (future integration) |
| Catering / event | Quote → deposit → event date → final payment |
| Pop-up / ticketed event | Capacity-capped events |
| Shipping — non-perishable | Dry goods where legally permitted |

System enforces capacity at checkout — overselling is a platform defect. See [Marketplace Mechanics — Fulfillment Models](../../product/marketplace-mechanics.md#fulfillment-models).

### Two-Sided Dynamics

**Supply-side funnel:** Apply → verify → first listing → first order → activation → retention via OS depth.

**Demand-side funnel:** Discovery → first order (trust surfaces dominate) → repeat purchase via favorites and notifications.

**Liquidity health signals:** Zero-result searches (supply gap), high views with low conversion (trust friction), cart abandonment at fulfillment (scheduling UX), creator concentration (early market or ranking issue).

## Technology Overview

### Architecture Approach

Marketplate launches as a **modular monolith** — a single deployable application with strict internal module boundaries mapping 1:1 to domain services. This optimizes early team velocity and operational simplicity while preserving a clear extraction path to independent services when load, team size, or failure isolation demands it.

**We do not start with microservices. We start with boring, readable modules that behave like services.**

Domain modules:

| Module | Responsibility |
|--------|----------------|
| **Identity** | Users, sessions, roles, permissions |
| **Trust** | Verification, moderation, audit |
| **Catalog** | Menu items, storefronts, availability, capacity |
| **Order** | Cart, checkout, order lifecycle, fulfillment state |
| **Payment** | Charges, refunds, Connect accounts, payouts |
| **Discovery** | Search, browse, ranking, collections |
| **Notification** | Email, push (future), in-app (future) |
| **Admin** | Platform config, operator actions, dispute tooling |

Trust layer enforcement is cross-cutting — verified-to-sell gates, transparency pre-payment, immutable audit logs, human approval on high-stakes actions. Full architecture: [Architecture Overview](../../engineering/architecture-overview.md).

### Technology Stack

| Layer | Choice |
|-------|--------|
| Primary database | PostgreSQL |
| Cache / sessions | Redis |
| Object storage | S3-compatible |
| Payments | Stripe + Stripe Connect |
| Search | PostgreSQL full-text initially → OpenSearch at scale |
| Runtime | Containerized services |

Architectural principles: one service one responsibility, documented APIs before implementation, observable by default, fail closed on trust, idempotent side effects. See [Engineering Philosophy](../../company/company-philosophy.md#engineering-philosophy).

### AI Platform

Four documented AI systems assist operations without replacing human accountability:

| System | Purpose | Human gate |
|--------|---------|------------|
| [Verification Assist](../../ai/verification-assist.md) | Document extraction, fraud flags | Human approves every verification decision |
| [Moderation Assist](../../ai/moderation-assist.md) | Policy violation scoring | Human enforcement decision |
| [Support Assist](../../ai/support-assist.md) | FAQ search, ticket classification | No autonomous customer replies |
| [Discovery Ranking](../../ai/discovery-ranking.md) | Trust-weighted ranking | Hard trust gates; auditable weights |

Governing principle: **AI recommends. Humans approve.** Platform overview: [AI Platform](../../ai/README.md).

## Operating Philosophies

All philosophies trace to the [Founding Constitution](../../company/constitution.md). When philosophies conflict, **trust wins** — then clarity, then long-term scalability. Decision frameworks: [Company Philosophy](../../company/company-philosophy.md).

### Product Philosophy

Every product decision optimizes for: Trust · Quality · Clarity · Long-term scalability · Beautiful design · Operational excellence · AI-first workflows · Documentation-first development · Maintainability · Human-centered experiences.

Build fewer things. Build them exceptionally well. Quality bar: if Apple, Airbnb, Stripe, and Etsy collaborated on a food marketplace, the result should feel like Marketplate.

**Decision filter:** Does this increase verifiable trust? Does it serve independent food creators specifically? Can a cottage food operator use it without support? Is it documented before implementation?

### Documentation Philosophy

**Documentation is production code.** Incomplete documentation means the feature is incomplete.

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Documentation precedes implementation | Spec before code |
| Traceability | Every engineering decision links back to documentation |
| Dual audience | Understandable by humans and AI agents |
| Link, don't duplicate | Prefer cross-references over repeated content |
| Evergreen | Written for years of consumption |
| Related systems | Every page references connected systems |

This repository is Marketplate's **institutional memory** — the operating system of the company.

### Engineering Philosophy

Optimize for **readability, maintainability, and modularity**. Prefer boring technology over clever technology.

- Every service owns one responsibility
- Every API documented before implementation
- Every module independently testable
- Every feature observable — logged, measurable, versioned, documented

Significant architecture decisions require ADRs in [`decisions/`](../../decisions/). Engineering docs: [`engineering/`](../../engineering/).

### Design Philosophy

Interfaces should feel **calm**. Whitespace is a feature. Motion communicates state. Consistency builds trust.

- One primary action per screen
- Premium feel: large photography, minimal interface
- Accessibility first (WCAG AA minimum)
- Responsive across desktop, tablet, and mobile

Design system execution: [`design-system/`](../../design-system/).

### Operations Philosophy

Every customer interaction should **reduce uncertainty**. Every incident becomes documentation. Every SOP should eventually become partially automated.

Every operational workflow requires: owner, trigger, SLA, AI responsibilities, human responsibilities, escalation rules, audit logging, post-mortem for trust-impacting incidents.

Operations docs (Phase 4): [`operations/`](../../operations/).

### Trust Philosophy

**Trust is the product.** Never sacrifice trust for growth.

| Pillar | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| Food safety | Non-negotiable compliance and verification |
| Identity verification | Creators are real, verified people |
| Kitchen verification | Production environments are verified |
| Transparency | Customers see what they need to trust |
| Reviews & community | Social proof and accountability |

Trust decision tree and anti-patterns: [Company Philosophy — Trust Philosophy](../../company/company-philosophy.md#trust-philosophy).

### AI Philosophy

AI should **not replace people**. AI removes repetitive work, improves consistency, proactively detects problems, and assists internal teams.

| Trust Impact | AI Role | Human Role |
|--------------|---------|------------|
| **High** (verification, moderation, compliance) | Recommend with confidence score | Approve or reject every decision |
| **Medium** (support triage, content suggestions) | Draft or classify; auto-act above threshold with audit | Review samples; handle exceptions |
| **Low** (internal search, doc generation) | Auto-act with logging | Periodic evaluation |

Every AI system documents: purpose, inputs, outputs, evaluation, fallbacks, confidence thresholds, security, human review, continuous improvement.

## Mission Filter for Every Team

Every team applies the mission through a simple filter:

1. **Does this help an independent creator run a better business?** If the primary beneficiary is a delivery aggregator or corporate restaurant chain, it does not belong on our roadmap.
2. **Does this increase trust?** Features that add complexity without increasing verifiable trust are deprioritized.
3. **Is the quality bar world-class?** Creators deserve software that feels as polished as products from Apple, Stripe, and Airbnb.
4. **Does this scale without sacrificing clarity?** We build fewer things and build them exceptionally well.
