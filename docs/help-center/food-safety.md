# Food Safety & Allergens

> Help Center · Food safety  
> Route: `/help/food-safety`  
> Last updated: 2026-07-03

## Overview

Marketplate does not prepare food. Creators disclose ingredients and allergens per menu item; you review this information before paying. Kitchens may handle multiple ingredients — Marketplate cannot guarantee allergen-free preparation even when a creator labels an item allergen-free.

This article covers allergen disclosures, checkout acknowledgment, severe allergy guidance, and how to report food safety concerns. Deep link anchor: `#allergens` on the in-app Help page.

---

## Step-by-step guidance

### Step 1: Review allergens before ordering

1. On each [Menu item detail](../../pages/customer/menu-item-detail.md), read the **Contains** and **May contain** allergen fields.
2. Creators are required to disclose major allergens per platform standards.
3. Dietary tags (gluten-free, vegan) are creator-provided — verify ingredients if you have restrictions.

[Screenshot: Menu item detail with allergen section showing "Contains: wheat, eggs, dairy" and ingredient list]

### Step 2: Set dietary preferences (optional)

1. In [Account settings](../../pages/customer/account-settings.md), set allergen flags under **Dietary preferences**.
2. Preferences highlight items during browsing but **do not replace** per-item review at checkout.
3. See [Account](account.md) for preference management.

[Screenshot: Browse results with allergen preference warning icon on items containing flagged allergens]

### Step 3: Acknowledge at checkout

1. If your order includes flagged allergens or you have saved allergen preferences, checkout requires acknowledgment.
2. Read: *By placing this order, you confirm you have reviewed the allergen information provided by the creator.*
3. Add order notes to restate severe allergies — creators see notes before production.

[Screenshot: Checkout allergen acknowledgment checkbox with expandable item-level allergen summary]

### Step 4: Communicate severe allergies

1. For life-threatening allergies, restate in checkout notes AND message the creator via [Messaging](messaging.md) before production starts.
2. Ask directly about cross-contact practices in their kitchen.
3. When in doubt, do not order — no platform can guarantee zero cross-contact in shared kitchens.

[Screenshot: Order notes field at checkout with example text about peanut allergy and cross-contact question]

### Step 5: Report a food safety concern

1. If you experience illness, allergic reaction, or believe food was mislabeled, stop consuming the product.
2. For **allergic reaction**, seek medical care first, then contact support via **Food safety** category immediately.
3. For labeling or handling concerns without emergency, message the creator and report via support.

[Screenshot: Contact support form with Food safety category selected and priority banner explaining response time]

## FAQs

### Does Marketplate guarantee allergen-free food?

No. Creators prepare food in kitchens that may handle multiple ingredients. Review disclosures, communicate with creators, and avoid ordering when cross-contact risk is unacceptable for your needs.

### What allergens must creators disclose?

Platform standards require disclosure of major allergens per applicable regulations. Exact requirements vary by jurisdiction — creators must complete compliance verification. See [Verification](verification.md).

### What if I have a severe peanut allergy?

Restate in checkout notes, message the creator before production, and ask about cross-contact. Consider ordering only from creators whose kitchens align with your safety requirements. When uncertain, do not order.

### How are mislabeled allergens handled?

Confirmed mislabeling triggers immediate creator suspension pending investigation per the [trust enforcement ladder](../../product/marketplace-mechanics.md#trust-enforcement-ladder). Report via Food safety category.

## Escalation guidance

**Allergic reaction or medical emergency:** Seek medical care first. Then contact support — **Food safety** category, **critical priority** (1-hour response target).

**Food safety concerns (non-emergency):**
- Suspected mislabeling, illness after consumption, unsafe handling
- **Support category:** Food safety
- **Expected response:** Within 4 hours
- **Include:** Order number, creator name, item(s), symptoms/timeline, photos of packaging or label, medical attention sought (yes/no)

Do not consume remaining product if safety is in question — retain for investigation if safe to store.
