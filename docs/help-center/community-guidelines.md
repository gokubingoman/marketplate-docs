# Community Guidelines

> Help Center · Community guidelines  
> Route: `/help/community-guidelines`  
> Last updated: 2026-07-03

## Overview

Marketplate is a community of verified creators and customers who value trust, quality, and respect. These guidelines define expected conduct for everyone on the platform — in orders, messages, reviews, and storefront content. Violations may result in warnings, restrictions, or account removal.

Guidelines complement binding terms in [`legal/`](../../legal/) *(Phase 5)* and enforcement procedures in [Platform policies](platform-policies.md).

---

## Step-by-step guidance

### Step 1: Treat others with respect

1. Communicate professionally in [Messaging](messaging.md) — even during disputes.
2. No harassment, threats, discrimination, or hate speech toward customers, creators, or support staff.
3. Food is personal — assume good intent first; escalate bad behavior to the platform.

[Screenshot: Messaging interface with community guidelines link in footer and report button on message bubble]

### Step 2: Be honest and accurate

**Creators must:**
- Disclose allergens and ingredients accurately — see [Menus & catalog](menus.md)
- Represent food photos truthfully — no misleading staging
- Honor stated policies on cancellation, pickup, and delivery
- Not claim verification status they have not earned

**Customers must:**
- Provide accurate delivery addresses and contact info
- Leave honest [Reviews](reviews.md) from verified purchases
- Not extort creators with review threats

[Screenshot: Review submission with guideline reminder banner about honest verified feedback]

### Step 3: Use the platform as intended

**Prohibited:**
- Off-platform payment to bypass fees or verification
- Fake orders or review manipulation
- Scraping creator data for spam lists
- Impersonating another person or business
- Selling prohibited items or operating without verification

[Screenshot: Checkout warning when message contains off-platform payment keywords with policy link]

### Step 4: Protect privacy

1. Do not share other people's personal information publicly in reviews or messages.
2. Creators receive customer contact info only for order fulfillment — not for unrelated marketing.
3. Report privacy violations via [Contact support](contact-support.md) — Trust & safety.

[Screenshot: Report concern form with Privacy violation category selected]

### Step 5: Report violations

1. Use **Report** on reviews, messages, or storefronts.
2. Or contact support → **Trust & safety** with specifics: who, what, when, evidence.
3. Do not retaliate — platform handles enforcement.

[Screenshot: Report flow confirmation with case reference number and expected review timeline]

## Enforcement ladder

Progressive enforcement per [Marketplace Mechanics](../../product/marketplace-mechanics.md):

```
Education → Warning → Listing restriction → Order suspension → Account suspension → Permanent removal
```

Immediate suspension examples: confirmed allergen mislabeling, fraudulent identity, credible threats, off-platform payment fraud at scale.

## FAQs

### Can creators refuse service?

Creators manage their business within guidelines. Refusal based on protected characteristics is prohibited. Capacity and safety refusals must be applied consistently and documented.

### Are jokes or strong language allowed in reviews?

Reviews must not contain harassment, hate speech, or threats. Strong criticism of food quality is allowed if factual and from verified purchase.

### What happens after I report someone?

Trust & Safety reviews within 24 hours for most reports. Outcomes may not be shared in detail to protect privacy — you receive confirmation your report was received.

### Can I appeal a guideline enforcement action?

Yes. Appeal instructions included in enforcement notification email. See [Platform policies](platform-policies.md).

## Escalation guidance

Report conduct violations via **Trust & safety** category — do not wait for order completion if safety is at risk.

**Expected response:** Within 24 hours (faster for threats or active harassment)  
**Include:** User/creator name, order number if applicable, dates, screenshots, description of violation

**Emergency threats:** Contact local authorities first, then report to Marketplate with police report number if available.
