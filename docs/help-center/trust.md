# Trust & Verification Badges

> Help Center · Trust  
> Route: `/help/trust`  
> Last updated: 2026-07-03

## Overview

Trust is Marketplate's product. Before you pay, you see who makes your food, where it is produced, and evidence that the creator completed platform verification. Badges are not marketing decorations — each maps to a documented review process with human approval on high-stakes decisions.

This article explains what verification badges mean, what they do not guarantee, and how to evaluate creators who display them. Creators applying for verification should read [Creator verification](verification.md). Deep link anchor: `#verification`.

---

## Step-by-step guidance

### Step 1: Find badges on creator profiles

1. Open any [Creator storefront](../../pages/customer/creator-storefront.md).
2. Badges appear in the profile header and on discovery cards in [Search](../../pages/customer/search.md) and [Browse](../../pages/customer/browse.md).
3. Tap or hover a badge for its definition — text labels are always visible, not icon-only.

[Screenshot: Creator storefront header with Verified Identity and Verified Kitchen badges with text labels]

### Step 2: Understand Verified Identity

**Verified Identity** confirms the creator is a real person or legal entity authorized to operate.

- Government ID reviewed
- Business registration where applicable
- Tax identity and contact verification
- Re-verification on material identity changes

This establishes **accountability** — you know who stands behind the order. It does not alone prove food safety.

[Screenshot: Verification badge glossary modal defining Verified Identity with bullet points of what was checked]

### Step 3: Understand Verified Kitchen

**Verified Kitchen** confirms where food is produced meets platform documentation standards.

- Production address confirmed
- Facility registration and inspection records where available
- Photos of production environment
- Every SKU linked to a verified location

Kitchen verification is **not** a substitute for government health inspection. Authorities retain regulatory authority. Marketplate verifies documentation and consistency.

[Screenshot: Kitchen transparency section on storefront showing kitchen type, general location, and verification date]

### Step 4: Read reviews and compliance status

1. Scroll to **Reviews** — only from verified purchases; moderated for fraud and policy violations.
2. Check compliance status if displayed — food handler certificates and licenses current.
3. Read the creator's story, policies, and fulfillment details before ordering.

[Screenshot: Reviews section with star distribution, verified purchase badges on reviews, and creator response example]

### Step 5: Report trust concerns

1. If a badge seems misrepresented, listing details contradict verification, or you suspect fraud, report via the storefront **Report concern** link or [Contact support](contact-support.md).
2. Select **Trust & safety** category.
3. Do not confront creators about suspected fraud in messaging — report to the platform.

[Screenshot: Report concern flow with category selection and description field]

## What badges do and do not mean

| Badge | Means | Does not mean |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| **Verified Identity** | Real, accountable operator | Every order will be perfect |
| **Verified Kitchen** | Documented production location approved | Government health grade; allergen-free kitchen |
| **Verified purchase review** | Reviewer actually bought the item | Creator paid for positive reviews |
| **High rating** | Consistent verified customer satisfaction | Immunity from occasional mistakes |

Unverified creators **cannot accept paid orders**. If you encounter a listing without verification badges accepting payment, report immediately.

## Common issues

| Issue | Likely cause | What to do |
|-------|--------------|------------|
| Badge missing on active storefront | Display bug or policy violation | Report concern; do not pay if badges absent |
| Kitchen address vague | Privacy policy — full address may post-purchase or at pickup | Check order confirmation; message creator if needed |
| Reviews seem fake | Platform prohibits fake reviews | Report via Trust & safety; reviews require verified purchase |
| Creator claims "Marketplate certified safe" | Misstatement — no such badge | Report; see accurate badge definitions above |

## FAQs

### Can creators pay to remove bad reviews?

No. Reviews are never suppressed for payment — ever. See [Reviews](reviews.md) and [Marketplace Mechanics](../../product/marketplace-mechanics.md).

### What happens to creators who violate trust policies?

Progressive enforcement: education → warning → listing restriction → order suspension → account removal. Confirmed allergen mislabeling triggers immediate suspension pending investigation.

### How is Marketplate different from anonymous delivery apps?

You buy from verified creators, not anonymous vendors. Identity, kitchen, and compliance layers work together — removing one weakens the model. See [Getting started](getting-started.md).

### Can I filter search to verified creators only?

Yes. Use the verification filter on [Search](../../pages/customer/search.md). Unverified creators are not ranked alongside verified supply.

## Escalation guidance

Report trust concerns via **Trust & safety** category:

- Suspected fraudulent identity or fake badges
- Creator operating outside verified kitchen
- Coordinated fake reviews or review extortion
- Harassment or safety threats

**Expected response:** Within 24 hours (elevated for fraud in active transactions)  
**Include:** Creator name, storefront URL, order number if applicable, specific concern with dates and screenshots

Active order fraud risk: also contact support via **Order issue** for transaction hold request.
