## Overview

Your menu is your product catalog — items, descriptions, photos, pricing, allergens, and production kitchen linkage. Accurate menus protect customers, reduce disputes, and meet compliance requirements. Every SKU must link to a verified production location.

This article is for **creators** managing catalog content. Customers browse menus on [Creator storefronts](../../pages/customer/creator-storefront.md) and [Menu item detail](../../pages/customer/menu-item-detail.md).

---

## Step-by-step guidance

### Step 1: Access your catalog

1. Open [Catalog](../../pages/creator/catalog.md) in Creator OS.
2. View items by status: Active, Draft, Sold out, Suspended.
3. Select **Add item** to create a new menu entry.

[Screenshot: Creator catalog list with item thumbnails, prices, status badges, and "Add item" button]

### Step 2: Create a menu item

1. Open [Menu item editor](../../pages/creator/menu-item-editor.md).
2. Enter name, description, price, category, and photos.
3. Link to **Verified kitchen** — required for paid listings.
4. Set lead time, batch limits, and fulfillment eligibility (pickup, delivery, catering).

[Screenshot: Menu item editor with photo upload, price field, kitchen linkage dropdown showing verified locations only]

### Step 3: Disclose allergens and ingredients

1. Complete **Contains** and **May contain** allergen fields — mandatory.
2. List ingredients accurately. Jurisdiction rules may prohibit certain items under cottage food — platform enforces category restrictions.
3. Dietary tags (vegan, gluten-free) are optional but must be truthful — mislabeling triggers enforcement.

[Screenshot: Allergen disclosure section with required allergen checkboxes and ingredient list textarea with validation warnings]

### Step 4: Manage availability

1. Mark items **Sold out** when inventory depletes — do not accept orders you cannot fulfill.
2. Use batch limits and capacity settings in [Availability](../../pages/creator/availability.md) for weekly menus.
3. Schedule item visibility for seasonal or pop-up menus.

[Screenshot: Item availability toggles with sold-out switch, batch quantity field, and schedule publish date picker]

### Step 5: Review before publishing

1. Preview customer view on menu item detail.
2. Confirm price, allergens, photos, and kitchen linkage.
3. Publish — item appears on storefront when verification and compliance are current.

[Screenshot: Item preview side-by-side with editor showing customer-facing menu item detail render]

## Common issues

| Issue | Likely cause | What to do |
|-------|--------------|------------|
| Cannot publish item | No verified kitchen linked | Complete [Verification](verification.md); link item to approved kitchen |
| Item suspended | Compliance expired or category violation | Check compliance center; adjust category or renew docs |
| Customer reports allergen issue | Disclosure incomplete or inaccurate | Update immediately; report to support if recall needed |
| Overselling batch item | Capacity not configured | Set batch limits; mark sold out promptly |
| Photo rejected | Quality or misleading content | Retake per photography standards; accurate representation required |

## FAQs

### Can I offer the same item from two kitchens?

Each SKU links to one verified production location. Create separate SKUs if the same dish is produced at different verified sites.

### How do custom and catering items work?

Enable custom order flag; set deposit and balance terms disclosed at checkout. See [Payments & fees](payments.md) customer article for deposit expectations.

### What items are prohibited?

Varies by jurisdiction — especially cottage food restrictions on refrigerated items, meat products, etc. Platform blocks prohibited categories per compliance rules. See [Platform policies](platform-policies.md).

### Can I change price after orders placed?

Price changes apply to new orders only. Existing confirmed orders honor checkout price.

## Escalation guidance

Contact support for:

- Item incorrectly blocked by category rules you believe are wrong for your jurisdiction
- Bulk catalog import assistance (if available)
- Urgent allergen correction after customer report — also use internal ops channels

**Support category:** Creator verification  
**Expected response:** Within 2 business days (faster for active allergen correction)  
**Include:** Item SKU, kitchen ID, compliance jurisdiction, rejection message screenshot

Confirmed allergen mislabeling: expect immediate listing suspension pending investigation per trust enforcement policy.
