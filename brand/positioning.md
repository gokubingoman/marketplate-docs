# Positioning

> Where Marketplate sits in the market, who we serve, and how we frame the competition.

**Status:** Active  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last updated:** 2026-07-03

## Positioning Statement

**For independent food entrepreneurs** — chefs, bakers, meal prep operators, caterers, food trucks, cottage food producers, and local food creators — **who want to build trusted businesses, not just take orders,**

**Marketplate** is the **trusted marketplace and operating system**

**that provides verified identity, food-native operations, and a premium customer experience**

**unlike delivery aggregators and generic e-commerce platforms,**

**because Marketplate is infrastructure built for creator trust — where verification, transparency, and long-term customer relationships come first.**

This statement traces directly to our [mission and trust thesis](../company/constitution.md#about-marketplate).

---

## Category Definition

We define our category as:

> **Trusted food creator infrastructure** — the software layer that lets independent food businesses operate, sell, and grow with the same credibility as established brands.

We do not compete in the **food delivery** category. Delivery apps solve a logistics problem for restaurants. Marketplate solves a **trust and operations** problem for independent creators.

| Dimension | Delivery apps | Marketplate |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Primary buyer | Hungry customer seeking convenience | Creator building a business; customer seeking trusted local food |
| Unit of value | Speed, selection, discount | Verification, repeat relationships, operational excellence |
| Seller identity | Restaurant brand (often buried) | Creator brand (always primary) |
| Revenue model | Commission + delivery fees | Creator-aligned platform economics *(see product strategy)* |
| Trust model | Star ratings, opaque | Verified identity, kitchen, compliance — visible and auditable |
| Relationship | Transactional, app-mediated | Direct creator–customer, platform-enabled |

## Competitive Frame

### Primary frame: Not delivery apps

When customers or investors compare us to Uber Eats, DoorDash, or Grubhub, we reframe:

- **They** aggregate restaurants for delivery convenience.
- **We** give independent creators the tools to run verified businesses and sell directly to their communities.

We are complementary, not competitive, with local pickup and creator-managed fulfillment. Creators may use Marketplate **instead of** listing on delivery apps — not because we deliver faster, but because they keep their brand, their margins, and their customer relationships.

### Secondary frame: Creator commerce platforms

| Competitor type | Examples | Our edge |
|-----------------|----------|----------|
| General marketplace | Etsy, Facebook Marketplace | No food-native verification, safety, or fulfillment workflows |
| Website builders | Shopify, Square Online | Generic commerce; creator must assemble food-specific compliance alone |
| Social selling | Instagram Shops | Discovery without trust infrastructure or operational depth |
| Restaurant POS + online ordering | Toast, Square | Built for restaurants with fixed locations, not independent/cottage creators |

### Tertiary frame: Local food discovery

Farmers markets, community boards, and word-of mouth remain competitors for **discovery**. Marketplate wins by combining discovery with **transaction, verification, and repeat purchase infrastructure**.

## Target Audiences

### Primary: Independent food creators

**Who they are**

- Home-based cottage food operators
- Meal prep and weekly menu businesses
- Bakers, pastry chefs, and confectioners
- Personal chefs and small catering operations
- Food truck and pop-up operators
- Commercial kitchen tenants building direct-to-customer brands

**What they need**

- Professional presence without enterprise software complexity
- Order management, scheduling, and fulfillment built for food
- Verification that increases customer confidence
- Tools to grow repeat customers, not one-off discount hunters
- Compliance support for local food regulations

**What they fear**

- Platform fees that erode margins
- Losing customer relationships to a marketplace
- Liability and food safety exposure
- Looking amateur next to chain restaurants on delivery apps

**How we reach them**

- Creator success stories with verification proof points
- Partnerships with commercial kitchens, culinary schools, cottage food associations
- Content on "building a trusted food business," not "getting more orders"

---

### Secondary: Local food customers

**Who they are**

- Health-conscious households seeking meal prep
- Families supporting local creators
- Food enthusiasts who value provenance and craft
- Office managers ordering catering from verified local sources
- Dietary-restriction-aware buyers who need accurate labeling

**What they need**

- Confidence in who made the food and where
- Clear allergen, ingredient, and pickup information
- Reliable ordering without delivery-app chaos
- Discovery of creators they can return to

**What they fear**

- Food safety incidents from unverified sellers
- Misleading photos or descriptions
- Hidden fees and unclear pickup logistics
- Anonymous sellers with no accountability

**How we reach them**

- Creator-led marketing (creators bring their customers)
- Local SEO and "verified local food" positioning
- Trust-forward brand campaigns — not discount-led acquisition

## Messaging Hierarchy

Use this order in external communications:

1. **Trust** — Verified creators, transparent operations
2. **Creator** — Real people, real kitchens, real craft
3. **Quality** — Premium experience, not discount marketplace
4. **Simplicity** — World-class software that stays out of the way
5. **Community** — Local relationships, repeat customers

Avoid leading with features, pricing, or comparisons to delivery app speed.

## Anti-Positioning

Statements we avoid:

- "The Uber Eats for home cooks" — wrong category, wrong incentives
- "Fastest food delivery" — not our product
- "Cheapest meals near you" — race to the bottom, erodes trust
- "Anyone can sell food" — verification is core; openness comes after trust gates
- "Disrupting restaurants" — we empower creators, not attack incumbents
